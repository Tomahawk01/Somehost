FAQ

Hosting is a service that allows you to host your information on a third-party server. In addition to the classic understanding that hosting hosts websites, there may also be databases, cloud file storage, mailboxes, ftp accounts and much more. A quality hoster ensures that your site is always available, will work without interruption and at a stable level. There are three types of hosting - virtual hosting, virtual server, dedicated server. Each service involves providing disk space, RAM and CPU power to the user to rent for a certain period of time: in the case of virtual hosting - the declared part of the server, located next to the sites or files to other customers, virtual server - the environment with far more resources, isolated from the other clients, a dedicated server - a separate physical resource, completely owned by one client at the time of rent.

Take into account two components - budget and potential attendance. If your resource is just starting out, in this case, the most economical option - a virtual hosting, where your site is located on the same server as the other customers. This is a good choice if the project is just starting or is not designed for a wide audience. In case you are not a beginner and understand that the site will need more resources, then it is worth choosing between the virtual and dedicated servers. To choose hosting - is not only to choose the right rate, adjust to the budgetary framework and decide on the CMS system, an important role in this matter plays the company-hoster - a reliable and trusted provider will provide quality service sites and provide maximum speed minimizing the potential problems.

If you have a website, you will need to upload it to hosting in order for it to be online all the time. Or if at one point you realize that you are not satisfied with the speed of your site on the current hosting, and frequent failures prevent normal operation, then you should think about migrating to another provider. Uploading a website to hosting can be done in several ways - by using different FTP-clients (FileZilla, Total Commander and others), through the File Manager in your control panel (cPanel, ISP and others) or by migrating a website using SSH. In each case, you need to be careful when archiving files and their further placement.
